Barrio Escalante

The walkable, food-and-coffee corner of San José

The most talked-about neighborhood in the city — gastronomy, cafés, and life on foot.

Barrio Escalante isn't a drive-through neighborhood; it's one you live on foot. Calle 33 holds a tight cluster of cooks, baristas and wine shops that moved in over the last decade and turned the block into San José's most active food strip. The mix is unusual: a quiet residential grid with real urban offerings — bakeries open early, restaurants run late, and there are parks, bookstores, a century-old church and a school in between.

The location works because it connects: 10 minutes to downtown San José, close to UCR and UNED, and with quick access to the east-side highways. Living here means most days you don't need a car.
